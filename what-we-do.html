WHAT WE DO

GlassProtech Public Transport

GlassProtech™ works extensively with public transport operators and authorities throughout Australasia and provides cost effective solutions to the problem of glass scratch vandalism and acid etching. Our range of anti-graffiti films provides a low cost method protecting glass, information screens or any flat non-porous surface. GlassProtech™ films are used by transport operators and authorities to:

  • Maintain a clean glass image
  • Provide a safe and secure environment for customers
  • Reduce the cost of glass damage
  • Help comply with local regulations on graffiti management

GlassProtech Architecture and Infrastructure

GlassProtech™ has a wide range of laminates and surface protection products that are specified by architects and used by construction companies.

The GlassProtech™ range of window films includes anti-graffiti films that protects glass from scratch graffiti and acid etching, security films that provide added protection against theft and injury and cutting edge energy saving films using the latest nano technology. Our huge range of Decorative films offer designers imaginative ways of creating fabulous spaces.

GlassProtech Heavy Machinery and Construction

GlassProtech™ surface protection films are used extensively by construction, mining, forestry and haulage companies to protect operators from the risks associated with the environment they work in. Protective film also limits the damage to polycarbonate screens and glass panes that can result in critical equipment being taken out of service for repairs.

Application of GlassProtech™ film can protect operators from breaking glass, protect polycarbonate screens, restore damaged windows and polycarbonate to an almost clear appearance.

GlassProtech Technology

GlassProtech™ is constantly monitoring and evaluating new film technologies that address issues for our customers.

Developing a method to print images onto anti-graffiti film has proven to dramatically reduce the instances of etching on internal windows of buses, bus and rail stations.

Film-based technology is a fast moving medium for the delivery of services. Both nano technology and the rapidly developing Graphene are allowing manufacturers to build very powerful services into a very thin film.
